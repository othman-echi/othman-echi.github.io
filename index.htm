Profile

Algebra, topology, combinatorics, and their interfaces.

Othman Echi is Professor of Mathematics in the Department of Mathematics at King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals. His work connects algebraic structures with topology and discrete mathematics, with sustained contributions to spectral spaces, prime spectra, primitive words, digital topologies, and Korselt-type number theory.

Research

Core areas

01

Commutative Algebra

Dimension theory, prime spectra, valuation theory, catenarian pairs, and related ring-theoretic structures.

02

General and Spectral Topology

Sober spaces, spectral spaces, quasihomeomorphisms, Skula spaces, compactifications, and foliations.

03

Combinatorics on Words

Primitive words, primitive deficiency, Hamming-compatible distances, and Levenshtein spheres.

04

Number Theory and Discrete Mathematics

Korselt numbers and sets, alternating series estimates, digital topology, and applications to theoretical computer science.
