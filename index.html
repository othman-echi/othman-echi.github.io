Profile

Number theory, algebra, topology, words, and inequalities.

Othman Echi is Professor of Mathematics in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Tunis El Manar, Tunisia. His work connects number theory, commutative algebra, topology, combinatorics on words, and mathematical inequalities, with sustained contributions to prime spectra, spectral spaces, primitive words, Korselt-type number theory, and estimates for alternating series.

Research

Core areas

01

Number Theory

Korselt numbers and sets, Kronecker polynomials, Williams numbers, and arithmetic questions around composite numbers.

02

Commutative Algebra

Dimension theory, prime spectra, valuation theory, catenarian pairs, and related ring-theoretic structures.

03

Topology

Spectral spaces, sober spaces, quasihomeomorphisms, Skula spaces, compactifications, foliations, and digital topologies.

04

Combinatorics on Words

Primitive words, primitive deficiency, Hamming-compatible distances, products of words, and Levenshtein spheres.

05

Mathematical Inequalities

Sharp estimates and hypergeometric methods for remainders of alternating series.
